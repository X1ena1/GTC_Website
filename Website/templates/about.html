About the Energy Incentive Rebate Program

Our Mission

The University of Hawai'i Energy Incentive Rebate Program is committed to fostering a sustainable campus environment by incentivizing energy-efficient upgrades across all departments. Our goal is to reduce the University's overall utility consumption, lower operational costs, and reinvest savings into academic and student services.

What is an Energy Rebate?

An energy rebate is a financial incentive offered to University departments to cover a significant portion (typically 30% to 50%) of the cost associated with installing approved, energy-saving equipment. These incentives are funded by internal energy savings pools and are designed to make high-efficiency projects immediately financially viable.

Key Objectives:

  • **Cost Reduction:** Dramatically lower the upfront capital required for major infrastructure upgrades (HVAC, Lighting, Water Systems).
  • **Sustainability:** Accelerate the replacement of old, inefficient systems with modern, eco-friendly alternatives, supporting the University's carbon neutrality goals.
  • **Budget Freedom:** Generate long-term utility savings that can be redirected back to the department's core educational or research missions.

How to Participate

Participation is open to all University departments. The process is streamlined through this system:

  1. **Browse Opportunities:** Review the **Opportunities page** for current eligible rebates (e.g., LED lighting, Chiller upgrades, Smart Controls).
  2. **Submit Application:** A contractor or department submits a formal application detailing the project and estimated savings.
  3. **Review & Approval:** The Facilities Management review board assesses the technical merits and financial viability.
  4. **Rebate Disbursement:** Upon project completion and verification, the pre-approved rebate funds are disbursed.
